Master Thesis on Extremal Combinatorics

The construction of extremal families of graphs is a task often done with the aid of ideas and theorems from different areas of mathematics sometimes not immediately related to combinatorics.
The methods used in this thesis are borrowed from algebraic geometry and finite geometry and can be summarised as follows. Let $V$ be a set of points inside a vector space $\mathbb{K}^d$ of dimension $d$ over a field $\mathbb{K}$. Define a graph $G$ whose vertex set is $V$ and the edge relations are given by the pairs of vertices vanishing at a polynomial function
$$f:(\mathbb{K}^d)^2 \to \mathbb{K}$$ (in this thesis we will take a billinear form). In order to gather useful geometric information we look through the theoretical lens of finite geometry, but we will also need the assistance of powerful algebraic methods to obtain some quantitative bounds on our arguments. As the graph we aim to build are finite we will use the finite fields $\mathbb{F}{q}$. However in order to exploit the algebraic geometric tools, it is requiered for us to work over an algebraically closed field. As we need to include our finite objects we will analyse our phenomenae over the algebraic closure $\overline {\mathbb{F}{q}}$ of our finite field.
